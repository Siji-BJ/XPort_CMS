xPort Business case

The above scenario presents bigger challenges for conventional ports that fall in tier 2 and tier 3 bracket where processes are mostly manual and investments are lesser to come by for the most modern equipment and resources. Being mostly government-run, under trans-national trade obligations, ports are highly regulated and are inherently slow movers. But the advent of digital technologies is a boon today and seems the quickest way to leapfrog and secure the business with high customer satisfaction and surge ahead of the competition. Putting the above scenario in perspective; ports are mostly owned by the state, though private ports are becoming the new norm in many countries. So, their role is generally that of the landlord providing services to port customers such as shipping lines, terminal operators, freight forwarders, customs, etc.

With the annual growth in global container traffic hovering around 4%, and mergers & acquisitions being the norm, with very little investments going into greenfield port projects, improving port efficiencies seem the way forward to avoid losing business to competing ports around.

Action is of high intensity in terminal operations (majority containerized) where automation is common or mandatory, though the level of automation varies. On the contrary, automation is quite insufficient in port operations management from the landlord front and that is where the opportunity exists for significant improvements via

  • Integration of port internal functions and
  • Integration of all external stakeholders to the port as well.

The later for simplicity is termed as Port Community Systems PCS

If the landlord is proactive and can evaluate the merits in port automation and take up full integration of external stakeholders as well, this will go a long way in enhancing the economic fortunes of the state, as ports play a major role in the national GDP.

International Maritime Organisation is a strong advocate for PCS and it has redefined the model as Maritime Single window, the name itself indicating efficiency, compliance and heightened customer service. This opportunity was the trigger for the genesis of a platform plan called xPort®; the brainchild of a team adept in maritime and digital technology space.

The suite of applications on the xPort platform was developed by practitioners with decades of maritime business background based in Rotterdam - the mecca for ports & seafarers; and India, backed by a large technology team that was there from the beginning of the digital era in IT in 2008 when mobile phones started getting smart and IoT made its way to making isolated data points integrated and inclusive for smarter management of enterprise and consumer systems. And today, this capability has gathered further momentum with cloud, AI, ML and analytics pave the way for smartness in maritime business that can be like never before.
